Froehde

Froehde's most important use is in identifying 5-APB and 6-APB, which are often misrepresented as MDMA and are difficult to identify with other reagents. Froehde may also be useful in identifying other new psychoactive substances. Contains enough reagent for approximately 50 tests.

Mandelin

Mandelin reagent tests for the presence of MDxx, ketamine, PMA/PMMA, speed, cocaine and more. Great standalone kit due to its ability to test for the presence of cocaine and ketamine. Contains approximately 50 tests.

Mecke

Good for detecting 2c-T-x compounds and certain opiates. Also produces clearly different color reactions for DXM and MDMA.

Marquis

Marquis reagent tests for the presence of MDxx, DXM, 2C-B, speed and more. Common standalone kit. Contains enough reagent for approximately 50 tests.

Liebermann

Liebermann Reagent is not recommended for use as a standalone kit for identifying MDMA. In conjunction with our other reagent kits, Liebermann can help identify certain cathinones (“bath salts”) and other new psychoactive substances. Contains enough reagent for approximately 50 tests.

Ehrlich

Ehrlich's Reagent can be used to positively identify LSD, helping rule out 25i-NBOMe, a highly toxic and extremely dangerous drug that is often misrepresented as LSD. Ehrlich's can also be used to identify other indoles (such as DMT, AMT, Psilocybin, etc.). Contains enough reagent for approximately 50 tests

Simon

Simon’s Reagent is a secondary test that tells the difference between MDMA and MDA. It can also differentiate amphetamine from methamphetamine. Contains two bottles.Simon’s reagent should never be used alone. It is only useful after first testing your sample with Marquis, Mecke or Mandelin.

Folin

Our Folin Reagent kit is a secondary test that tells the difference between MDMA, MDA and MDE. It also detects piperazines such as BZP and TFMPP. This should NOT be used as a standalone kit. Contains two bottles with enough reagent for approximately 50 tests.
